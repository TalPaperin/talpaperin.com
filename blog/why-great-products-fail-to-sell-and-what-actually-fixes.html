Why Great Products Fail to Sell , And What Actually Fixes It

By Tal Paperin· April 24, 2026· 7 min read
B2B SalesRevenue Leadership

I've spent 20 years in B2B sales across four continents.

Large companies and corporations - PepsiCo. Del Monte. Mars. MassChallenge... But also dozens of startups and growth-stage companies in between.

And in all that time , across all those companies, all those industries, all those markets , I've seen the same thing happen over and over again.

A brilliant product. A passionate founder. A team that believes in what they're building.

And sales that simply don't work.

Not because the product is bad. Not because the market doesn't exist. Not because the founder isn't smart enough or hungry enough or working hard enough.

Because no one built and implemented the system.

The $500,000 Problem Nobody Talks About

When a company reaches the point where sales become a real problem, the standard solution is to hire.

Hire a CRO. Hire a VP of Sales. Hire consultants. Build a team.

And if you have $500,000 a year to spend on sales leadership , that works.

But most founders don't. Most growth-stage companies don't. And even the ones who do , often hire the wrong person, wait 6 months for results, and end up back where they started.

So what actually happens?

The founder keeps selling. Burning time they don't have. Making it up as they go. With no playbook, no scripts, no methodology , just instinct and persistence.

Or they hire reps who have no idea what to say, because nobody ever built the playbook they need.

Or they bring in a consultant at $10,000-$20,000 a month who gives them a deck and disappears.

I've seen all three. I've been called in to fix all three.

And every single time, the root cause is the same:

No sales infrastructure.

The 8 Reasons Your Sales Aren't Where They Should Be

After 20 years, I can tell you exactly why deals don't close. It's never random. It's always one of these:

1. You don't know exactly who to sell to , and who to ignore. No clear ICP. You're pitching everyone and converting nobody.

2. You don't know what to say, word for word. You know your product. You don't have a script. There's a difference.

3. You can't handle your competitors when they come up. And they always come up. If you don't have a battle card, you're improvising.

4. You fumble objections instead of owning them. "Let me think about it" kills more deals than bad products ever will.

5. Your reps aren't trained on YOUR product, YOUR buyer, YOUR market. Generic training produces generic results.

6. Your outreach is generic and gets ignored. If your cold email could have been written by anyone, it will be read by no one.

7. You have no real-time deal support. Every call, every email, every negotiation , you're on your own.

8. You can't afford a CRO to fix any of it. So the problems compound. Month after month.

Sound familiar?
