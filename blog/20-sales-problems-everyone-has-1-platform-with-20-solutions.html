20 Sales Problems EVERYONE has - 1 Platform with 20 solutions.

By Tal Paperin· June 7, 2026· 7 min read
B2B SalesStartups

Most B2B founders are not bad at selling. They are selling without a system.

No playbook. No one to ask before a hard call. No process for the day. And no real answer to the most important question of all: who should we actually be selling to?

A great Chief Revenue Officer fixes every one of those problems. A great CRO also costs around $500,000 a year, which is exactly what an early-stage company does not have.

That gap is why we built TheDealMentor.AI: a Virtual CRO. Part AI, part real human revenue leader. It gives founders and small sales teams the strategy, the documents, the daily process, and the in-the-moment coaching that normally only well-funded companies can buy. All of it is built on proven methodology (Sandler, SPIN, Challenger, Gap Selling, Never Split the Difference) and grounded only in your real business data, so it never invents numbers or fake case studies.

Here is the problem-by-problem breakdown.

"I have a product, not a go-to-market plan."

Most founders can describe what they sell but cannot show you the path to revenue. Your GTM Plan fixes that first. It defines your one-liner, your market, the buyers to chase versus avoid, your edge, your go-to-market motion, the revenue math, a 90-day plan, and the biggest risks. It is the strategic frame that every other document hangs from.

"I'm not even sure who my real buyer is."

Chasing the wrong customer is the most expensive mistake in sales, and the hardest to see from the inside. ICP Segments give you one saved, editable list of your ideal buyer profiles. Better still, the platform runs live web research to pressure-test your ICP: it tells you whether your target looks right, partially right, or likely wrong, checks who comparable companies actually sell to, validates your geography, and even finds the competitors you did not list. You stop guessing about your market.

"Every call is improvised."

Winging it does not scale, and it does not close. Your Sales Playbook is the full operating manual: positioning, the sales process, discovery questions, three field-ready pitches, objection handling, closing, follow-up cadences, competitive positioning, your recommended sales tech stack, and how to arm a champion to sell internally for you.

"I can't sell one prospect at a time forever."

Outbound alone has a ceiling. Your Marketing Plan shows how to get in front of buyers at scale: your growth model, where your buyers actually spend attention, your highest-ROI channels, the offer and message that pulls them in, budget tiers, your marketing tech stack, and the metrics that matter. It is honest about which channels fit your business and which do not.

"I don't know what to actually do each day."

Strategy is useless without execution. Your Daily Workflow is the literal click-path: the morning setup, the source-to-sequence-to-call-to-follow-up routine, a time-boxed day and week, pipeline hygiene rules, and the numbers that tell you whether a day was productive.

"I freeze in the moment, and objections kill my deals."

This is where most coaching fails you, because the moment passes before help arrives. Sales Copilot is not one feature. It is eight tools for the exact moment you are in:

  1. Call Guide - a tailored plan for your next call, and you can practice it against an AI version of your buyer before the real one.

  2. Objection Handler - classifies the objection, then gives you the words to say, a follow-up, and a walk-away line.

  3. Competition Analysis - a full battle card for any competitor you are up against.

  4. Email Analyzer - what the prospect's message really means, and the reply that moves the deal.

  5. Follow-Up - the message that actually gets a response.

  6. Proposal Builder - a complete, tailored proposal draft.

  7. CRM Notes - a clean, structured summary of the call in seconds.

  8. Account Research - company and person intel, and your specific way in.

Every one pulls from your intake and your chosen ICP segment, so it fits your deal, not a template.

"My cold outreach gets ignored."

Generic sequences get deleted. Outreach Builder is six sequence builders, each tailored to a specific ICP segment and built around real pain:

  1. Cold Email - a multi-touch outbound sequence.

  2. LinkedIn - connection plus message sequence.

  3. Social Posts - content that pulls buyers to you.

  4. Referral & Intro - warm-path intro asks.

  5. Re-Engage - win back prospects who went cold.

  6. DM / WhatsApp - short, text-first outreach.

"Deals stall and I have no one to ask."

A pipeline without next steps is just a list of hope. My Deals lets you track each deal and log your notes, calls, and emails, and the CRO reads the deal, names the risk, and tells you the next move.

"I need an answer right now, not next week."

Here is the part no one else is doing. The Ask the CRO advisor is on every single screen, always. It knows your generated program, your active deals, and how the platform works, so you can ask "what do I do on this deal" or "where is X" and get a real answer in seconds. And when the moment is bigger than a chat, you have a real, live human CRO one click away.

Think about that. An AI revenue leader that never sleeps, sitting next to a real one you can book whenever the stakes are high. Not a chatbot. Not a course. Not a coach you see once a month. Both, in one platform. No one is doing anything like this.

"Generic AI doesn't know my business."

It does here. Upload your pricing decks, case studies, and competitor docs to the Knowledge Base, and the platform pulls from them in every output. The more it knows, the more specific everything becomes.

For sales teams: "New reps take six months to ramp, and everyone pitches differently."

Managers get the whole company program in one click: Sales Playbook, Why-Buy, Marketing, Vertical Intel briefings, SDR and AE guides, and ICP-specific scripts for cold calls, voicemails, gatekeepers, and objections. Then you certify reps, let them practice in simulation, and publish materials so the whole team sells one consistent, proven way. Ramp time goes from quarters to weeks.
