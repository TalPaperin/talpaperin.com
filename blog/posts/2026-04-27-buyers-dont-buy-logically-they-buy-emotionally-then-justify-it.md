Then they use logic to explain what they already decided.

Trump gets this better than anyone.

One sentence. And suddenly everyone feels something. Rage, excitement, disbelief. Doesn't matter. He triggered a reaction.

You might hate him for it. Fine.

But if you don't understand what he's doing you can't sell.

Your buyer isn't analyzing your deck. They're not reading your feature list. They're sitting across from you or skimming your email and they're feeling something. Or they're not.

And most of you? You're explaining.

"AI-powered." "End-to-end." "Scalable solution." "Revenue optimization."

Nothing. No feeling. No tension. No reason to care.

So they say "looks interesting" and you never hear from them again.

I've watched brilliant founders lose deals they should have won. Not because the product was weak. Because the message was dead.

No emotion. No urgency. No relevance.

You don't need to be Trump to fix this. You don't need to be provocative or controversial.

You just need to make your buyer feel something real.

Pain. Urgency. Fear of falling behind. Desire to win.

That's the job.

I've rebuilt messaging and sales motions for B2B companies across three continents. The pattern is always the same great product, dead message, dying pipeline.

That's exactly where I start as a Fractional CRO.

If your deals are dying at "looks interesting" DM me. That's my specialty.
