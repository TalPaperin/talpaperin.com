<div><h2>But do they really need one?</h2><p>A lot of founders and CEOs secretly hate their sales costs.</p><p>Not publicly, of course.</p><p>Publicly, everybody talks about “growth,” “scale,” “go-to-market strategy,” and “building world-class sales organizations.”</p><p>But behind closed doors?</p><p>Many CEOs and founders look at their sales department and think:</p><p>“How are we spending THIS much money and still struggling to close deals?”</p><p>And honestly, sometimes that frustration is completely justified.</p><p>Especially after:</p><ul><li><p>bad VP Sales hires</p></li><li><p>inflated pipelines</p></li><li><p>missed forecasts</p></li><li><p>SDR teams producing noise instead of meetings</p></li><li><p>endless sales meetings with no real execution</p></li><li><p>burning hundreds of thousands of dollars without meaningful revenue growth</p></li></ul><p>I’ve seen founders go through two or three sales leaders in under two years.</p><p>Not because all those people were terrible.</p><p>Because many companies simply are not ready for a traditional enterprise-style sales organization yet.</p><h2>The Real Cost of a “Real” VP Sales</h2><p>Most founders dramatically underestimate what a GOOD VP Sales or CRO actually costs.</p><p>And I don’t mean salary alone.</p><p>A truly experienced sales leader in today’s market can easily cost:</p><ul><li><p>$250K-$350K per year</p></li><li><p>plus bonuses</p></li><li><p>plus equity</p></li><li><p>plus commissions</p></li><li><p>plus recruiters</p></li><li><p>plus onboarding</p></li><li><p>plus tooling</p></li><li><p>plus the team they immediately need built around them</p></li></ul><p>Because the moment a VP Sales comes in, the machine starts growing.</p><p>Now you suddenly need:</p><ul><li><p>SDRs</p></li><li><p>AEs</p></li><li><p>CRM systems</p></li><li><p>outreach platforms</p></li><li><p>reporting structures</p></li><li><p>pipeline management</p></li><li><p>onboarding processes</p></li><li><p>sales enablement</p></li><li><p>compensation plans</p></li><li><p>forecasting systems</p></li><li><p>weekly meetings</p></li><li><p>dashboards</p></li><li><p>management layers</p></li></ul><p>And before the company even has a stable and repeatable sales motion, it’s already carrying enterprise-level overhead.</p><p>That’s where panic usually begins.</p><h2>The Problem Most Founders Don’t Want to Admit</h2><p>A lot of startups still haven’t fully figured out:</p><ul><li><p>who actually buys</p></li><li><p>why they buy</p></li><li><p>which messaging works</p></li><li><p>what objections kill deals</p></li><li><p>what their repeatable sales process even is</p></li></ul><p>But they still try to build mature sales structures anyway.</p><p>I’ve seen startups with:</p><ul><li><p>weak positioning</p></li><li><p>unclear differentiation</p></li><li><p>unstable pricing</p></li><li><p>inconsistent messaging</p></li></ul><p>…trying to operate like publicly traded SaaS companies.</p><p>So now the company has:</p><ul><li><p>dashboards</p></li><li><p>reports</p></li><li><p>meetings</p></li><li><p>forecasts</p></li><li><p>“pipeline”</p></li><li><p>activity metrics</p></li></ul><p>But not necessarily revenue.</p><p>One of the biggest startup killers I’ve seen over the last 20 years has nothing to do with product quality.</p><p>It’s premature sales infrastructure.</p><p>Companies build enterprise sales overhead long before building enterprise revenue.</p><h2>The VP Sales Trap</h2><p>Here’s the uncomfortable truth:</p><p>Many founders hire a VP Sales hoping that person will magically solve uncertainty.</p><p>But uncertainty is not solved by management layers.</p><p>It’s solved by:</p><ul><li><p>understanding the market</p></li><li><p>sharpening positioning</p></li><li><p>improving execution</p></li><li><p>closing deals</p></li><li><p>listening to buyers</p></li><li><p>fixing messaging</p></li><li><p>refining process</p></li></ul><p>And ironically, many VP Sales hires end up spending more time:</p><ul><li><p>recruiting</p></li><li><p>managing</p></li><li><p>forecasting</p></li><li><p>building structure</p></li></ul><p>…than actually helping close deals.</p><p>That doesn’t mean VP Sales leaders are unnecessary.</p><p>Far from it.</p><p>Great sales leadership is incredibly valuable.</p><p>The problem is timing.</p><p>Many companies hire enterprise sales leadership far too early.</p><h2>The Market Is Changing</h2><p>The interesting part is that the market is slowly adapting to this reality.</p><p>Today companies have far more flexible options:</p><ul><li><p>freelance salespeople</p></li><li><p>outsourced SDR teams</p></li><li><p>external closers</p></li><li><p>fractional sales leadership</p></li><li><p>commission-only structures</p></li><li><p>full sales outsourcing models</p></li></ul><p>And honestly?</p><p>For many startups, those models make much more economic sense in the early stages.</p><p>Especially now.</p><p>The market became much less forgiving toward inefficiency.</p><p>Investors are paying more attention to:</p><ul><li><p>burn</p></li><li><p>execution</p></li><li><p>efficiency</p></li><li><p>revenue quality</p></li><li><p>operational discipline</p></li></ul><p>The old “grow headcount first and figure it out later” model is becoming dangerous.</p><h2>Why I Built My Model Differently</h2><p>Years ago, after serving as VP Global Sales &amp; Business Development myself, I kept seeing the exact same pattern again and again.</p><p>Good companies. Good products. Smart founders.</p><p>But completely overwhelmed by the cost and complexity of building a real sales organization.</p><p>Especially internationally.</p><p>So instead of forcing startups to build everything internally from scratch, I built a different model.</p><p>I step in as a Fractional CRO.</p><p>And under one roof, we already have:</p><ul><li><p>leadership</p></li><li><p>process</p></li><li><p>infrastructure</p></li><li><p>trained salespeople</p></li><li><p>execution systems</p></li><li><p>international business development experience</p></li></ul><p>Companies move faster without spending years building the machine alone.</p><p>Sometimes the smartest thing a company can do is NOT immediately build a massive internal sales hierarchy.</p><p>Sometimes they simply need experienced execution without enterprise-level overhead.</p><p>And honestly?</p><p>A lot of founders are finally starting to realize it now.</p></div>
