  <p>Israeli cosmetics - case study.</p> 
  <p>The Dead Sea is one of 7 World's Nature Wonders. Located in Israel, more than 400 meters below sea level, it's both local and an International tourist attraction.</p> 
  <p>Besides that, it is also a true gold mine. It has been harvested for minerals (phosphates and others) for various industries - and those have been exported globally - but this is not today's story.</p> 
  <p>Today we'll talk about an interesting episode in the Global Trade, International Business and Sales - and Local brand - using the Cosmetics industry as an example.</p> 
  <p>The "Dead Sea Cosmetics" is a huge industry, rolling billions of $ year - and, as the name suggests - it [should] originate from the Dead Sea. In Israel. There are dozens of brands, exporting, out of Israel, all over the globe, with "Ahava" being the most known.</p>

  <p>However, in the 21st century, being a "local" product / brand no longer means that the product has actually originated from the specified country. I first encountered this in Kiev, Ukraine, where I was trying to buy a traditionally embroidered shirt - and all those shirts where made in Turkey.</p> 
  <p>In today's Global world, costs of development and production are calculated - and impact the location of the manufacturing desiccation. This is known to all - but the unknown is the scale this has taken.</p> 
  <p>Dutch tulips are grown in Ethiopia. Israeli bell peppers are grown in Kenya. Korean cellphones are manufactured in Israel... And now - some of the "Dead Sea" cosmetics are made in:</p>
