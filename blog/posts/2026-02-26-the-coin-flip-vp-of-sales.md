Less punchline, more authority.</p><p>Here’s the expanded article version ,  still your voice, but structured and publishable.</p><hr><h2>The Coin Flip VP of Sales</h2><p>Have you ever heard something as ridiculous as this?</p><p>A company where the VP of Sales was chosen by a coin flip ,  and 40% of the “sales team” didn’t even know they were in sales.</p><p>I wish this was a metaphor. It wasn’t.</p><p>Three founders. Strong product. Smart people. Good funding. None of them wanted to own sales. So they flipped a coin. The “winner” became VP of Sales.</p><p>That was the foundation of the revenue engine.</p><h2>The Situation</h2><p>I was brought in because the company was burning cash and couldn’t close deals.</p><p>The leadership told me:</p><ul><li><p>“We have a sales team.”</p></li><li><p>“We have pipeline.”</p></li><li><p>“The market is slow.”</p></li><li><p>“We just need better execution.”</p></li></ul><p>I ignored the CRM. I don’t trust dashboards. I don’t trust pipeline stages. I don’t trust what CEOs <em>think</em> is happening.</p><p>I talk to the people. And so I talked to the sales guys. </p><p>Out of five “salespeople”:</p><ul><li><p>Only one had any formal sales training.</p></li><li><p>Two never wanted to sell in the first place and were just collecting a paycheck.</p></li><li><p>Two thought they were developers who occasionally “help with demos.”</p></li></ul><p>They didn’t even know they were considered part of the sales team.</p><p>This wasn’t chaos.</p><p>It was design.</p><h2>This Isn’t Incompetence. It’s a Belief System.</h2><p>What I saw wasn’t stupidity. It was ideology. And unfortunately, a very common one.</p><p>The belief that:</p><ul><li><p>Product matters more than sales.</p></li><li><p>Great features create revenue.</p></li><li><p>If the tech is good enough, customers will come.</p></li><li><p>Sales is something intelligent people can “figure out.”</p></li></ul><p>So developers become sales. Founders become VP Sales. Activity becomes “traction.” Meetings become “momentum.” CRM entries become “progress.”</p><p>And everyone feels better ,  until payroll approaches.</p><h2>The Comfort Theater</h2><p>Here’s the part most founders don’t like hearing:</p><p>Sometimes a sales structure isn’t built to generate revenue. It’s built to generate comfort.</p><p>Comfort for investors.</p><p>Comfort for the board.</p><p>Comfort for the founders themselves.</p><p>“Look, we have a team.” “Look, we hired a VP.” “Look, we’re doing outreach.”</p><p>But there is no architecture. No qualification discipline. No defined buyer strategy. No economic mapping. No decision-process control.</p><p>Just motion.</p><p>And motion feels productive ,  until cash runs out.</p><h2>Why This Happens</h2><p>Sales is the most emotionally uncomfortable function in a company.</p><p>It requires:</p><ul><li><p>Rejection.</p></li><li><p>Clarity.</p></li><li><p>Hard conversations.</p></li><li><p>Accountability.</p></li><li><p>External validation.</p></li></ul><p>The product is controllable. Sales is exposure.</p><p>So founders gravitate toward what they control... And they assign sales to whoever draws the short straw.</p><p>In this case ,  literally.</p><h2>The Cost</h2><p>When sales are treated as secondary:</p><ul><li><p>Pipeline inflates artificially.</p></li><li><p>Forecasts become optimism.</p></li><li><p>Burn rate continues.</p></li><li><p>Hiring decisions are based on hope.</p></li><li><p>Investors are reassured with activity.</p></li></ul><p>Then reality arrives.</p><p>Revenue misses. Runway shrinks. Confidence erodes. Emergency mode begins.</p><p>That’s usually when I get the call.</p><p>Sometimes it’s early enough to fix.</p><p>Often, the damage is structural.</p><h2>The Real Problem</h2><p>If your VP of Sales was chosen by convenience, politics, or avoidance ,  you don’t have a sales problem.</p><p>If your sales team doesn’t know they’re in sales, you don’t have a sales problem.</p><p>If activity replaces strategy, you don’t have a sales problem.</p><p>You have a leadership problem.</p><p>Revenue is not generated by titles. It’s not generated by features. And it’s definitely not generated by coin flips.</p><p>It’s generated by design.</p><p>And design requires ownership.</p><p>Reality always collects.</p><p>The only question is whether you face it early ,  or let it invoice you later.</p></div>
