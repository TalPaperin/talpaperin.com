A large part of the heimishe business world runs in Yiddish. The conversations, the handshakes, the trust, all of it in mame loshon. And most sales help from the outside world has no idea this world even exists, let alone how it works.

Let me be straight with you, because anything else would be against the point. I do not speak fluent Yiddish, and I will never pretend that I do. What I have is years of working closely inside the Yiddish-speaking, heimishe business world, and a real feel for how it operates.

## What actually matters more than the language

Plenty of people speak Yiddish and cannot sell. The thing that matters is understanding how this world buys and sells, and that I do know:

- Deals run on trust and reputation, built over time, often through a shared connection
- The kehilla is the network. A good name opens every door and a bad one closes them all
- The calendar is sacred. Shabbos, Yom Tov and the zmanim are not negotiable, and a sales process that ignores them is dead on arrival
- Discretion is everything in a world where everyone knows everyone

I work with Yiddish-speaking owners in English, and where the language matters in the actual selling, we make sure the right person is in the room. What I bring is the sales engine and the leadership, built with respect for the world you live in.

## The work

I take ownership of revenue the way a senior VP would: the strategy, the team, the pipeline and the number, hands-on. For heimishe and Yiddish-speaking businesses, that often means building a real selling machine where there was only word of mouth, and opening the wider market without losing the kehilla that built you.

## Why a frum operator and not just any consultant

Because you should not have to explain your world to the person you hired to grow your business. I am a frum, shomer Shabbos Yid. I understand the world. And I can sell.
