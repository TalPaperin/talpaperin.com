France is a large, proud market that rewards those who respect its language and its relationships, and quietly shuts out those who do not. I have built, hired, trained and managed sales teams in France, more than once and for several companies, and here is how to win it.

## Is France actually your market?

France is one of Europe's biggest economies, with strong industry, tech and public sectors. But it is not a market you enter in English from abroad. It demands real localization and a local presence. I help you judge whether the opportunity justifies that commitment, then build it, instead of half-entering and stalling.

## How French buyers really buy

French buyers expect to do business in French, full stop. They value relationships, intellectual rigor and a well-argued case, and they are wary of pure American-style hard selling. Decisions respect hierarchy, so you need the right level in the room. Trust and formality matter early; once you have earned them, the relationship is durable.

## What I build and run in France

- A French-speaking sales team, hired, trained and managed
- Positioning and materials properly localized, not translated
- A local entity when customers or hiring require it
- Tech support and customer service in French, to local expectations
- The CRM, pipeline, forecast and accountability

## How I would open France

Commit to the language and a local presence, or do not bother. Lead with a rigorous, well-argued case, build the relationships, and respect the hierarchy. I run it in the seat, hands-on.

## Common questions

**Do I need French-speaking salespeople?** Yes. France does business in French, and trying to sell in English from abroad signals you are not serious. I hire, train and manage the local team for you.
