Brazil is the giant of Latin America, a huge, warm, relationship-driven market that punishes outsiders who treat it casually. I have built and run sales operations in Brazil, more than once and for several companies, and here is how to do it properly.

## Is Brazil actually your market?

Brazil is by far the largest economy in South America, with deep industrial, agribusiness and tech sectors. The upside is enormous, but so is the complexity: Portuguese is essential, bureaucracy and tax are real ("custo Brasil"), and nothing happens without local presence and relationships. I help you judge whether you are ready, then build it.

## How Brazilian buyers really buy

Brazilians buy from people they like and trust, and that trust is personal and built face to face. They are warm and relationship-first, expect Portuguese, and are wary of a foreign vendor who shows up, pitches and disappears. Cycles can be long and bureaucratic. Invest in the relationship and a real local presence and Brazil rewards you with loyalty and scale.

## What I build and run in Brazil

- A Portuguese-speaking sales team, hired, trained and managed
- Positioning and an approach built for a relationship-first buyer
- Distributors and local partners where that is the right model
- Tech support and customer service in Portuguese, to local expectations
- The CRM, pipeline, forecast and accountability

## How I would open Brazil

Commit to Portuguese, local people and a real presence, build the relationships, and plan for bureaucracy and longer cycles. I run it hands-on, in the seat.
