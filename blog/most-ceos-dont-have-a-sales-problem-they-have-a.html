Most CEOs don't have a sales problem. They have a motion problem.

By Tal Paperin· June 4, 2026· 1 min read
B2B SalesRevenue LeadershipGo-To-Market

They have a motion problem. They don't know where to start... And where to go next (and how).

Their Salespeople (or they themselves) are calling the wrong people. The "ICP" is a guess they wrote down once and never validated. So the pipeline is full of people who'll never buy.

When a prospect names a competitor, they freeze. They have no answer for "why you instead of them." So the deal stalls and goes dark.

When someone says "we already have something" or "this isn't the right time," they have no response. So the meeting ends with "let me think about it."

Every deal is a one-off. Every rep starts from scratch. Every quarter is a coin flip.

Sales doesn't have to be hard. It just is when you're improvising every call without a system.

The point is the system, not the tools. Without the system, more tools just make the improvisation faster.
